# FUTURE_DS_01 – Business Sales Performance Analytics

## About the Project

In this project, I analyzed the Superstore Sales dataset to understand sales performance across different categories, regions, customer segments, and shipping modes. Using Power BI, I created an interactive dashboard to identify trends, generate insights, and support business decision-making.

## Project Objective

The main goal of this project was to:

* Analyze sales and profit performance.
* Identify top-performing products and categories.
* Compare sales across regions and customer segments.
* Study sales trends over time.
* Present findings through an interactive dashboard.

## Dataset

The project uses the Superstore Sales Dataset, which contains information about:

* Orders
* Customers
* Products
* Sales
* Profit
* Discounts
* Regions
* Shipping Modes

## Tools Used

* Power BI
* Microsoft Excel

## Data Preparation

Before creating the dashboard, I:

* Checked for missing values.
* Verified data types.
* Reviewed the dataset for duplicate records.
* Validated sales, profit, quantity, and discount fields.
* Organized the data for analysis and visualization.

## Dashboard Features

* Total Sales Overview
* Total Profit Overview
* Total Quantity Sold
* Total Discount Given
* Profit by Category
* Sales Trend Analysis
* Sales by Region
* Sales by Category
* Sales by Segment
* Sales by Ship Mode
* Top Selling Products

## Key Insights

* Technology generated the highest sales and profit.
* The West region recorded the highest sales performance.
* Consumer customers contributed the largest share of revenue.
* Standard Class was the most commonly used shipping mode.
* Furniture generated high sales but relatively low profit compared to other categories.

## Recommendations

* Focus more on high-performing Technology products.
* Strengthen marketing efforts in high-revenue regions.
* Review pricing and discount strategies for Furniture products.
* Continue optimizing Standard Class shipping operations.
* Explore opportunities to improve sales in lower-performing regions.
